## Summary

RFC0036 accepted with the rolling-policy option (operator): fix the POLICY ahead of time (goal, capacity, priority rule, stop conditions, cycle count N); at each boundary the loop closes the sprint (retro, lessons, close gate), fetches origin, regenerates the plan from the live backlog absorbing new findings and lessons, shows a dry-run preview, and continues. Opt-in; origin-drift refusal under --strict at every boundary. Lane partitioning deliberately excluded - its own follow-up CR.

## Impact

An operator wanting an evening of unattended delivery must return at every sprint boundary to re-plan; a frozen multi-plan queue would rot because the backlog is generated by the work - proven on this repo's own sprints.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] sprint accepts a standing policy (N cycles + goal/capacity/order/stop conditions); each boundary runs the close-down then regenerates the plan from the live backlog after a fetch
- [ ] A boundary whose close gate fails, whose origin diverged under --strict, or whose regenerated plan is refused STOPS the run with a handoff - never executes a stale or ungated plan
- [ ] Each cycle records its own forecast, sprint goal, retro and run-state - cycles are auditable as separate sprints
