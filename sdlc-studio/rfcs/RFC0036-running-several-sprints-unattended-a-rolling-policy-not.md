# RFC-0036: Running several sprints unattended: a rolling policy, not a queue of frozen plans

> **Status:** Draft
> **Decomposed-into:** CR0320, CR0321
> **Affects:** .claude/skills/sdlc-studio/scripts/sprint.py, .claude/skills/sdlc-studio/scripts/loop_guard.py
> **Date:** 2026-07-14
> **Created-by:** sdlc-studio file
> **Raised-by:** sdlc-studio; agent; v1

## Summary

Operator request: plan several sprints ahead, then say "run the next four" and have the loop work through them autonomously, fetching from origin at each boundary. Optional, because a multi-user project may clash - and potentially a way to allocate work to different teams.

The autonomy is right. The QUEUE is the part to think hard about.

A sprint plan made four sprints ahead is stale before it runs, because the backlog is not a fixed queue that work drains - it is GENERATED BY the work. Evidence from the sprint that prompted this: a 5-unit batch was planned, and delivering it RAISED eight new artefacts (BG0133-BG0140, CR0261, RFC0035). BG0133 did not exist at plan time and turned out to be the most important unit of the week: without it the estimator could not be falsified at all. A frozen sprint 2 would not have contained it. The learning loop sharpens the problem: each plan reads the lessons of the sprint before it, and a pre-baked sprint 3 cannot read lessons that do not exist yet. It would plan with its eyes shut, and do so with the same confident output as a good plan - which is the exact false-authority CR0260 was built to abolish.

THE PROPOSAL: separate the POLICY (fixed ahead of time) from the PLAN (regenerated at each boundary).

"Run the next four sprints" becomes four plan -> execute -> close cycles under a standing policy: the goal, the capacity, the priority rule, the stop conditions. The plan is recomputed at each boundary from the backlog as it actually is, having absorbed the previous sprint lessons, the newly raised findings, and any origin drift. Autonomy without staleness.

TEAM ALLOCATION is the strong half of the idea, and the machinery already exists. CR0260 derives SHARED-FILE CLUSTERS from `Affects`. A backlog partitioned into lanes with NO FILE IN COMMON cannot collide by construction - which is a mechanical basis for handing lane A to one team or agent and lane B to another. The same computation that currently WARNS about a false-parallel wave can ALLOCATE. It caught CR0254/CR0260 sharing sprint.py on its first run.

MULTI-USER SAFETY is half-built: sprint plan already runs a git fetch and an origin-drift pre-flight (warns when behind, refuses under --strict).

## Design Options

- **Rolling policy (RECOMMENDED). Fix the policy ahead of time (goal, capacity, priority rule, stop conditions); regenerate the plan at each sprint boundary from the live backlog, after a fetch and after absorbing the previous sprint lessons and findings. Autonomy without staleness. Cost: the operator cannot see the exact batches in advance, only the rule that will choose them.**
- **Frozen queue of N plans. The operator sees and approves every batch up front. Maximum predictability, but the plans rot: any finding raised in sprint 1 cannot enter sprint 2, and the lessons loop is dead for the whole run. Rejected unless a hard audit requirement demands pre-approved batches.**
- **Hybrid: a frozen queue that is REVALIDATED at each boundary and REFUSES to run a batch whose units have changed status, gained a dependency, or been superseded by a newly raised finding. Keeps the operator preview, and fails loud rather than silently executing a stale plan. More machinery than the rolling policy for the same safety.**

## Recommendation

Rolling policy, with lane partitioning by shared-file cluster as a separate, independently useful capability. Keep the operator preview by showing what the NEXT plan would be (a dry run) rather than freezing four. Fetch and origin-drift check at every boundary, and make the whole thing opt-in, since a busy multi-user repo is exactly where a long unattended run is most likely to collide.
